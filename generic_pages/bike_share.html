Data Analytics Capstone

How does a bike-share navigate speedy success?

This is one of the capstone projects from the "Google Data Analytics Specialization" course, and it is my first time working with Tableau. Hence, the visual might not look appealing, but it does the job to showcase the insights.

Details: In this case study, I had to be a junior data analyst for Cyclistic, a bike-share company operating in Chicago. The company has a fleet of 5,824 geotracked bicycles and 692 stations across the city. The bikes can be rented from one station and returned to any other station within the system.


Task: To complete the task, I kept three key questions in mind:

  1. How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?
  2. Why would casual riders buy Cyclistic annual memberships?
  3. How can Cyclistic use digital media to influence casual riders to become members?

Action: I started by downloading and reviewing the data files, ensuring that the data was clean and processed properly for analysis. Then, following the case study guidelines, I performed data analysis using spreadsheets. To provide a comprehensive understanding of the data, I created a dashboard using Tableau, which allowed for better visualization and sharing of insights.

Results: Based on the analysis, here are the key insights:

  • The most popular spot for both casual riders and annual members to start their bike rides is Streeter Dr and Grand Avenue.
  • The number of registered members significantly exceeds the number of casual riders, with a difference of nearly 500,000.
  • Classic bikes are the preferred choice for both casual riders and annual members.
  • The summer season shows the highest demand for bike rides.
  • Casual riders tend to ride more on weekends, while annual members have consistent usage throughout the week.

From these insights, I attempted to answer the following questions:

  1. How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?
    The insight that registered members outnumber casual riders by a significant margin reinforces the difference in usage between the two groups. It suggests that annual members have a more frequent and consistent utilization of Cyclistic bikes compared to casual riders.
  2. Why would casual riders buy Cyclistic annual memberships?
    The insight that the most popular spot for both casual riders and annual members to start their bike rides is the same indicates that casual riders might find value in the convenience and accessibility of the bike-share system. They may realize that purchasing an annual membership offers them the flexibility to easily rent and return bikes from various locations, making it a cost-effective option for their regular cycling needs.
  3. How can Cyclistic use digital media to influence casual riders to become members?
    The insight that summer is the most popular time to ride cycles aligns with the marketing strategy for Cyclistic. By utilizing digital media platforms, Cyclistic can target casual riders with targeted advertising and promotions during the summer season, highlighting the benefits of becoming an annual member. Emphasizing the availability of bikes, popular starting locations, and the overall positive experience during this peak period can effectively influence casual riders to consider purchasing annual memberships.

Conclusion: These insights provide valuable information for Cyclistic to understand the behavior and preferences of their customers. By aligning their marketing efforts with the usage patterns and preferences identified in the analysis, Cyclistic can tailor their messaging and incentives to encourage casual riders to transition into becoming loyal annual members.
